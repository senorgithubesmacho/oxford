Syllabus

Course purpose

Learn how to plan, build, and present digital media projects with clear deliverables, clean workflow, and evidence of process. The goal is to produce work you can show, not just turn in.

What you will practice

  • Story-first thinking: intent, audience, and message.
  • Production basics: assets, timelines, versions, and exports.
  • Professional habits: naming, folders, checkpoints, and feedback loops.
  • Reflection: proof of work and what you learned.

Edit these bullets anytime to match the current quarter.

Grading (simple version)

  • Project Statement (clarity, feasibility, alignment).
  • Proof of Work (drafts, tests, iterations, checkpoints).
  • Final Deliverable (quality, meets spec, export correct).
  • Show It (presentation + explanation of choices).
  • Assess It (reflection: what changed, what improved).

Core rules

  • Do your own work; collaboration must be clearly credited.
  • Keep files organized; missing/incorrect exports count as missing.
  • Ask early when stuck; do not wait until the last day.
